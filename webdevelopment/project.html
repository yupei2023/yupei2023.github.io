Project: VirtualGeo

Topic: Blending Mixed Reality and Generative AI to Teach Geography: An MR+GenAI Learning Environment

This study examines “VirtualGeo”, a Mixed Reality and Generative AI platform designed to enhance U.S. geography knowledge among international students. By integrating immersive technologies, VirtualGeo allows students to engage with spatial content within an interactive digital landscape. Findings show significant gains in international students’ geographic understanding and underscore VirtualGeo's potential to support adaptive, student-centered learning. Implications suggest VirtualGeo as a scalable tool for online training, promoting broader access to geography and other spatial disciplines.

Limitations & Future Directions

  • Small sample limits generalizability
  • Self-reported data may introduce bias
  • Usability issues: discomfort, eye strain
  • Long-term retention not assessed
  • Future: larger samples; AR versions for wider access

Project: iVRLab

Topic: Enhancing Student Self-Efficacy and Interest in Microelectronics through Immersive Virtual Reality

This study investigates iVRLab, an immersive VR microfabrication training system, and its influence on college students’ self-efficacy and interest in microelectronics during a one-hour workshop. iVRLab simulates photolithography cleanroom operations, providing hands-on virtual experiences that merge theory with practice. A pre–post analysis revealed a significant rise in self-efficacy (M = 4.11 → 4.37, p = 0.0066). Correlations among engagement, embodiment, and immersion underscore the value of embodied VR learning.

Limitations & Future Directions

  • Small, homogeneous sample
  • Brief, single-session design
  • No control group for comparison
  • Technical issues: lag, headset discomfort
  • Future: longitudinal studies, broader STEM applications

Project: AI-Virtual Patient Encounter

Topic: The Usability of an AI-Enhanced Virtual Standard Patient in Nursing Education

This study explores an AI-VSP system integrated with Meta Quest 2 to enhance nursing education. Addressing a nursing shortage, AI-VSPs offer scalable, cost-effective patient-encounter training. Usability testing with 63 nursing students yielded an SUS score of 81.6, indicating excellent usability. AI-VSP encounters create immersive practice opportunities, laying groundwork for future deliberate-practice research.

Significance

This work demonstrates a cost-friendly, adaptable AI-VSP solution for nursing programs. Educators can tailor patient “personalities” and difficulty levels without deep tech knowledge. The gamified interface appeals to modern learners and promises broader adoption in under-resourced institutions.
